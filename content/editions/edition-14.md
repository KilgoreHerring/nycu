- [The State of AI: How Organizations Are Rewiring to Capture Value](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/quantumblack/our-insights/the-state-of-ai#/)
	- What: McKinsey's State of AI report covers an overview of how artificial intelligence is being deployed across industries. The report highlights that 65% of organisations are now using GenAI, nearly doubling from last year. Adoption is highest in sectors like technology and finance, but legal and professional services are catching up. Despite this growth, 42% of companies cite legal and compliance risks as a major concern. The survey also highlights that CEO oversight of AI initiatives correlates with significant bottom-line impact, emphasising the importance of leadership in AI adoption.
	- So what: This shows that the wider market is catching up, with more and more clients starting AI journeys, giving us leverage to drive our lawyers forwards in the importance of this tech. Our team should be prepared to discuss how our tools can help elevate the efficiency and accuracy of work, both internally and in client conversations. Additionally, the rise in AI-related compliance concerns presents an opportunity for us to work closely with OGC to develop further guidelines and compliance materials to assist lawyers in using tools.
- [Cleary Gottlieb + Springbok AI: BigLaw Finally Gets It](https://www.geeklawblog.com/2025/03/cleary-gottlieb-springbok-ai-biglaw-finally-gets-it.html)
	- What: Cleary Gottlieb has acquired Springbok AI, an AI platform start up tailored for legal research, document review, and contract analysis. The plan is to integrate the tech seamlessly with Cleary's workflows, offering firm-specific insights and legal reasoning capabilities. It also comes with acquiring their data science and development team.
	- So what: This move signals a shift in how top-tier law firms approach AI: moving from off-the-shelf solutions to proprietary, firm-specific platforms. This reinforces the importance of tailoring AGPT and other AI-powered solutions to fit our firm's workflows. It also signals that clients may soon expect similar AI-enhanced services, making it essential for us to proactively communicate our AI capabilities and explore how we can integrate AI into client-facing offerings.
- [Agentic Document Review – White Paper](https://syllo.ai/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/Agentic-Document-Review-White-Paper-3-21-2025.pdf)
	- What: Syllo's white paper discusses the application of agentic AI systems in complex litigation document reviews. These systems coordinate multiple LLMs to autonomously manage document review tasks, addressing challenges such as context limitations and accuracy. The paper reports that this approach has achieved high recall and precision rates in real-world litigations, suggesting a significant reduction in discovery costs and time.
	- So what: This approach could significantly enhance efficiency in areas like M&A due diligence, contract analysis, and regulatory reviews. Given our work on Deal Clarity, we can explore how agentic AI review improves accuracy, automates repetitive tasks, and to integrate seamlessly with our existing workflows.
